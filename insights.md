# Key Insights – Gen-Z Fintech Analytics

## 1. Revenue Concentration (Pareto Effect)

Top 20% of users contribute the majority (~50%+) of total revenue.

**Insight:**  
Retention of high-value users is more impactful than acquiring new users.

---

## 2. Champions as Core Revenue Drivers

Champions generate disproportionate revenue despite being a smaller segment.

**Insight:**  
These users should be prioritised with premium experiences, rewards, and personalised engagement strategies.

---

## 3. At Risk Users as the Largest Growth Lever

At Risk users form the largest segment but contribute minimal revenue.

**Insight:**  
Even small improvements in reactivation can significantly increase overall revenue.

---

## 4. Lost Users Still Have Recoverable Value

Lost users previously had transaction activity and historical engagement.

**Insight:**  
Selective reactivation campaigns can recover hidden revenue from this segment.

---

## 5. Behavioural Patterns Drive Value More Than Demographics

User activity such as recency, frequency, and monetary value is a stronger indicator of user value than static attributes.

**Insight:**  
Segmentation should be behaviour-driven rather than relying solely on demographic data.

---

## Final Takeaway

- Retention is more valuable than acquisition  
- High-value users should be prioritised  
- Behavioural analytics enables better product decisions  

This aligns closely with real-world fintech growth and retention strategies.
